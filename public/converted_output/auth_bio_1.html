Eric Abrahamson is a professor of management at Columbia Business School, Columbia University. His groundbreaking and controversial work challenging conventional management wisdom has been published in many leading management journals, including the Harvard Business Review. He is the author of Change Without Pain: How Managers Can Overcome Initiative Overload, Organizational Chaos, and Employee Burnout (Harvard Business School Press, 2004). Eric speaks frequently to executive audiences around the world and has consulted to a wide variety of Fortune 500 and other organizations.

David H. Freedman is a contributing editor and the technology columnist at Inc. magazine. He is an occasional contributor to Newsweek and has written for the Harvard Business Review, the Atlantic Monthly, the New York Times, Science, Wired, and many other publications. Freedman’s most recent book is Corps Business: The 30 Management Principles of the U.S. Marines (HarperBusiness, 2000). He is also the coauthor, with Charles C. Mann, of At Large: The Strange Case of the World’s Biggest Internet Invasion (Simon & Schuster, 1997) and the author of Brainmakers: How Scientists Are Moving Beyond Computers to Create a Rival to the Human Brain (Simon & Schuster, 1994).
